Where we stand · CCC / Wake Up Cambridge perspective

More homes — about 14,000 of them — built the smart way.

We support more housing — actively, not grudgingly: on the order of 14,000 new homes by 2030, as real homes for every kind of household. Studios and one-bedrooms for singles and seniors; two-, three- and four-bedroom homes so families with children can stay; integrated, multigenerational buildings at every price point — not one product repeated 14,000 times.

Being thoughtful means three questions, asked every time. Where we develop: parking lots, underused commercial strips and transit corridors before occupied homes and tree-lined blocks. How we develop: with design standards, tree protection, graduated transitions, and real community space designed into large projects. What we develop: the mix of homes a whole city needs — because a process that is only a pipeline from land to dollar bills builds none of it.

WHERE Build where it's underbuilt

Parking lots, vacant parcels and transit corridors first — the Atlas maps exactly where they are.

HOW Keep Cambridge cohesive

Design standards, tree protection, graduated transitions, and civic space built into large projects.

WHAT The full mix of homes

Studios to four-bedrooms, mixed-use and mixed-income — integrative, multidimensional housing.

FOR WHOM Children, families, seniors

Judge every project by who can actually live there — new neighbors and the ones already home.
